Introduction

The term "Big 3" refers to three anime series that defined an entire generation of fans during the 2000s. These shows were not only long-running and commercially successful, but they also played a major role in expanding anime's global popularity.

Years later, a new wave of anime titles rose to international fame. With improved animation quality and streaming platforms making access easier, fans began referring to a modern trio as the New Big 3.

The Original Big 3

The Old Big 3 consisted of three legendary series serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump magazine:

  • One Piece - The ultimate pirate adventure
  • Naruto - The journey of a ninja chasing his dream
  • Bleach - A supernatural battle saga

These anime aired weekly for years, building strong fan communities and becoming household names across the world.

The Rise of the New Big 3

In the late 2010s, anime entered a new era dominated by seasonal releases and cinematic-quality animation. Streaming platforms allowed series to reach audiences instantly worldwide.

  1. Jujutsu Kaisen - Dark fantasy and curse battles
  2. Demon Slayer - Emotional storytelling with stunning visuals
  3. My Hero Academia - Superhero-inspired shonen action

These series quickly gained massive popularity and are often considered the spiritual successors to the original Big 3.

Key Differences Between the Generations

  • Old Big 3 were long-running weekly anime.
  • New Big 3 follow seasonal production models.
  • Modern animation quality is significantly higher.
  • Streaming platforms now dominate global distribution.
  • Social media amplifies modern fan engagement.

While both generations achieved massive success, they represent different eras of anime evolution.
